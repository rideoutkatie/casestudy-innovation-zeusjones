What's Stopping You?

Start small and socialize your plan.

Don’t oversell it — show it. And show it early. Make it tangible. Define what you want to make or do, assemble a small team, and transform that idea from a wall of Post-it® notes into a prototype – in weeks, not months. Use tools like Keynote or Sketch — or clay and cardboard — to create a low-fidelity version of your idea. Then walk around and show it to other people — maybe even a select group of customers. Ask for feedback and use what you hear to make it better. By moving quickly, modeling humility, and demonstrating your willingness to make improvements based on what you hear, you create a precedent that will help your company understand what innovation can do.
